Cooking

Cooking is not only a really fun hobby but it's a great way to feed myself and my family. I enjoy the thrill of cooking and making something new. I not only love the successes but the "happy little accidents" from following a recipe that I have never made before.

I learned how to cook as a teenager, my mom taught me how to make pasta with a can of tomato paste, sauce, sausage, and seasonings. I'm glad she did because the very first time I tried to cook potatoes, I sliced them and the put them into the microwave. Needless to say, I did not eat those potatoes. Here is one of my favorite recipes that I like to share and make. Since I am of Puerto Rican descent, cooking for me is a way to practice my culture.

Recipe

This is the first recipe that my father taught me how to cook and it's a staple in my house.

Arroz Con Pollo

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long grain white rice
  • 2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup sofrito
  • 1/2 onion chopped
  • 1 lb chicken thighs or chicken pieces of choice
  • 1/2-1 tsp cumin adjust to taste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • olive oil

Instructions

  1. Wash the chicken and add spices, mix well.
  2. Wash the rice and set aside.
  3. Fry the chicken in a pot until the chicken is brown on both sides, then remove chicken.
  4. Add sofrito to the pot and cook until fragrent, then add everything else to the pot.
  5. Add in the water, bring to a boil and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Add in chicken and cook for 25 minutes.
  7. Add fresh cilantro for garnish.

Retro Repair

Growing up in the late 90s, I was a big fan of having several gameboys because they were always fun to play and I could take them anywhere on the go.

One of the problems with them was the awful screens. You would have to be glued to a lamp or get an adapter to help illuminate the screens for you to play at night, which is when I got to play as a kid.

A few years ago, I was going through some of my old things in my parent's basement and I found my old gameboy. It bought back so many memories and I wanted to play with it again. Sadly it did not turn on and I decided to look into what I could do to fix it. This turned into a fixation and a hobby.

Gameboy Color

When doing research on my gameboy that didn't turn on, I disovered that there was a whole world of different repairs and modifications that I could do to give my gameboy color new life. I decided to do the following:

  • Get a new shell and buttons
  • Install a brand new IPS screen
  • Install a new speaker
  • Remove corossion from the motherboard.

Magic the Gathering

One of my favorite ways to spend time with friends is to play different games. Out of all of the games that I play is Magic the Gathering. I love playing magic for several reasons:

  • It's a very social game, so I can get out of the house and see people.
  • There are many different ways to play, with thought put into how you want to approach the game, including Aggressive, Control, or Combo.
  • It can be very methodical in the way that you play, which is how I play.
  • There are many ways to play magic, all of them being inventive.

One of the formats that I play is called Pauper. It's a way of playing Magic on the cheap, only using commons to make up your deck. Not only is it cheap, but since there aren't any huge power imbalances, you can be more stragetic.

I wanted to share one of the decks that I have created. It's called Dimir Terror. It has a control playstyle with something called tempo mixed in. The way you play the deck is that you use your Soceries and Instants to help you gain advantage and keep your opponent from achieving their game plan. When your graveyard fills up, you can take advantage by using the creatures in you deck, Tolarian Terror which it's casting cose is reduced the more Soceries and Instants are in the graveyard, and Gurmag Angler can use those cards and exile them from your graveyard to reduce it's casting cost.

Lastly, since this deck likes to draw a lot of cards, the final creature, Sneaky Snacker, can be played from the graveyard if you draw more than two cards a turn.
